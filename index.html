Analyzing Global Streaming Trends

DS4200 Final Project

Music has long served as a powerful form of expression, reflecting social change, cultural identity, and political movements. For this project, we analyze global streaming data from 2018-2024 to examine how music preferences vary across countries and regions, how genre popularity differs geographically, and how engagement behaviors vary between Free and Premium users. By studying these patterns, we aim to better understand how audiences interact with music worldwide.

About the Data

We use the Global Music Streaming Listener Preferences dataset from Kaggle, containing ~5,000 rows and 12 columns. The data includes listener demographics (Age, Country), streaming habits (Minutes Streamed Per Day, Number of Songs Liked, Listening Time of Day), top genre and artist preferences, subscription type (Free or Premium), and engagement metrics like Discover Weekly Engagement (%) and Repeat Song Rate (%). This dataset is suitable for trend analysis, predictive modeling, and visual exploration of global music streaming patterns.

Dataset source: Kaggle: Global Music Streaming Listener Preferences
